Activision Blizzard CEO Could Make a Fortune if Microsoft Acquisition Goes Through

By Juan Carlos Rey Medina Published on 2023-04-01

One of the most vocal public figures pushing for the closure of the Activision Blizzard deal with Microsoft is Bobby Kotick, who has criticized regulators on more than one occasion. These entities are the main reason why Microsoft's acquisition has not yet been finalized. However, there is a reason behind all his insistence: money.

According to Tweaktown, the CEO of Activision Blizzard could make a fortune if the Microsoft acquisition goes through. It would be a total of 185 million dollars, but he would not be the only one seeing his pockets filled with cash, as the top executives of Activision would also benefit from the purchase offer of $95 per share of the company.

Kotick has 4.3 million shares in Activision Blizzard, but he also has the right to acquire another 2.2 million, as commented by Stephen Totilo, a journalist at Axios. In addition, if you are one of those who thinks that Microsoft should withdraw from Great Britain, you should know that it would be losing more than 800 million dollars annually if it does not sell its games in that territory, something totally unfeasible, as discovered by TweakTown.

What happens if Bobby Kotick is fired?

The story changes a lot, and Bobby Kotick probably wouldn't mind being fired considering all the money he would make. He could earn more than 290 million dollars, but that's not all. If Microsoft fails in its attempt to acquire Activision Blizzard, it is very likely that Bobby Kotick will remain in his position at the company, as reported by FOX Business.
